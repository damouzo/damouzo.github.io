About Me

Hi! I'm Dani

I started my career in the wet lab, working with proteomics and discovering how much biological insight remains hidden inside large-scale datasets. That realization led me to transition into bioinformatics and data science, driven by a simple idea: complex biology deserves solutions that are measurable, scalable, and reproducible.

Today, I develop bioinformatic solutions for biomedical challenges. From multi-omic analysis in gene therapy to variant interpretation in cancer and cloud/HPC optimization. I enjoy collaborating with cross-functional teams, transforming raw data into actionable insights, and building pipelines that accelerate scientific impact.

Looking forward, I aim to continue connecting biology with engineering. I'm excited about automated and scalable workflows analysis that advance precision medicine. Whether you're looking for someone to strengthen your computational strategy or to join a project pushing the boundaries of therapeutic discovery, I'd love to build that future together.
